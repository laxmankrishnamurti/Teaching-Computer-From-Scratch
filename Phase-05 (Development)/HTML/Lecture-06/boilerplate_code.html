Autobiogaphy of a Mahatma Gandhi Mahatma Gandhi was born on 2nd October 1869 in Porbandar, Gujarat. He was a lawyer by profession and later became a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He is known for his philosophy of non-violence and civil disobedience, which inspired many movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. Gandhi led several campaigns for social reforms and played a crucial role in India's struggle for independence. He was assassinated on 30th January 1948. His legacy continues to inspire people around the world to fight for justice and equality through peaceful means. Gandhi's teachings on truth, non-violence, and self-discipline remain relevant today, making him one of the most influential figures in modern history. His birthday, 2nd October, is celebrated as Gandhi Jayanti in India and is also observed as the International Day of Non-Violence worldwide. Gandhi's life and teachings continue to be a source of inspiration for millions, and his commitment to truth and justice remains a guiding light for those who seek to create a better world.
